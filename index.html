Rooster.io

Live on a domain with a different name, rooster.io is a webfrontend for delivering schedules for the students and teachers of Werkman College Stadslyceum. What started as a small project from a couple of students years ago, has grown into the most used schedule site by students. This is my implementation of it.

Dropzone

Another one of my most polished projects. Dropzone is a small desktop application that hosts your files without a third-party with theoratical unlimited download speed. Just drag-and-drop your files directly into the app window and it will be instantly online. The app takes care of the hosting, port forwarding and streaming.

The files that are being shared can be previewed on a web frontend by the downloaders. Large media files like homemovies, music or cool gameclips can be instantly streamed through the webinterface as well.

yplayer

Ever wanted to just quickly listen to that one song without too much hastle, right from the commandline? Well, yplayer has got you covered. It's a small CLI script written with Node.js to quickly list, download and play music from one of the largest databases of music on this planet: YouTube. Just install via npm, insert your YouTube API Datakey and type "yplayer -s <your song>". It's that easy.
